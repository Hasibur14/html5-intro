About Mauntains

Mountains are landforms that rise above the surrounding land and sea level. They are steeper than hills and have a pointed top called a peak. Mountains can be divided into four main types: Upwarped, Volcanic, Fault-block, Folded (complex. Upwarped mountains are formed when pressure from under the Earth's crust pushes upward into a peak. Volcanic mountains are formed from eruptions of hot magma from the Earth's core. Fold mountains occur when two plates collide, shortening occurs along thrust faults, and the crust is overthickened.
